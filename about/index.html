About

I attended college at Virginia Tech, dual majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics, then earned a Master's in Applied Mathematics at Johns Hopkins University while working full time at Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab. My work there focused on sensor integration and exploitation, real-time video and image processing, computer vision, and tracking algorithms for radar and video. This job took me all over the USA, training people to use the software that I helped develop.

After 4 years in living in Baltimore, I left the US in early 2015 to explore the world, living for a year in Mongolia and now stationed in Tbilisi, Georgia, where I have been since 2016. During this time I worked remotely in web development, devops, systems architecture, and project management for various startups in the US and abroad.

I now consult for companies interested in understanding and utilizing blockchain technology. In my free time I contribute to Bitcoin Core and open source projects that further development of The Lightning Network.

When I am not working I enjoy exploring mountains, making natural mead, and sleeping in on rainy days. Work-life balance is my jam.
