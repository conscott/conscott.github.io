About

I grew up in the small town of Ferrum, VA: home to cows, corn, and moonshine. I attended college at Virginia Tech, dual majoring in Computer Science and Math, then earned a Master's in Applied Math at Johns Hopkins University while working full time at The Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab. My work at APL mostly focused on sensor integration and exploitation, real-time video and image processing, and tracking algorithms for radar and video.

After 4 years in Baltimore, I left the US in early 2015 to explore a bit of the world, living for a year in Mongolia and now stationed in Tbilisi, Georgia, where I have been since 2016. During this time I have worked remotely in web development, devops, and systems architecture for various startups in the US and abroad.

I now focus on contributing to Bitcoin and related projects and consult for companies interested in understanding and utilizing blockchain technology.

When I am not working, I enjoy hiking and exploring the Caucasus Mountains, making natural mead, trying all the food and drink the world has to offer, and sleeping in on rainy days.
