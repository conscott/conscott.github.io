About

I attended college at Virginia Tech, dual majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics, then earned a Master's in Applied Mathematics at Johns Hopkins University while working full time at Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab. My work focused on sensor integration and exploitation, real-time video and image processing, computer vision, and tracking algorithms for radar and video. I also flew all over the USA, installing our system and training personnel to use the software our group developed.

I left the US in early 2015 to explore the world, living (and working) for a year in Mongolia and for three years in Tbilisi, Georgia, which is an awesome city I recommend everyone visit. During this time I did remote work in web development, devops, systems architecture, and project management for various startups in the US and abroad.

I now live in the UK and focus on consulting and developing for companies interested in understanding and utilizing blockchain technology. In my free time I try to contribute to Bitcoin Core, lightning network, and other open source projects that I find interesting.

When I am not working I enjoy exploring mountains, making natural mead, and sleeping in on rainy days. Work-life balance is my jam.
