About

I grew up in the rural town of Ferrum, VA among the cows and corn fields, often walking along the local Appalachain Trail with my family. I attended college at Virginia Tech and majored in Computer Science and Math, then earned a Masters in Applied Math at Johns Hopkins University while working full time at The Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab. My work at APL mostly focused on sensor integration and exploitation, real-time video and image processing, and tracking algorithms for radar and video.

After 4 years in Baltimore, I left the US in early 2015 to explore a bit of the world, living for a year in Mongolia and now stationed in Tbilisi, Georgia, where I have been since 2016. During this time I have worked remotely in web development, devops, and systems architecture for various startups in the US and abroad. I now have a focus on Bitcoin Core development and consult for companies interested in understanding and utilizing blockchain technology.

I also love the math, hiking, and craft beer.
